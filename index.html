ABOUT ME

A native of Taiwan, I am a trained lifespan developmental psychologist. My program of research focuses on the psychological and motivational processes that promote healthy and optimal adult development and aging. Currently, I am a postdoctoral research associate in the Movement Neuroscience and Rehabilitation Lab. My work has appreaered in The Gerontologist, Psychology and Aging, and Quality of Life Research.

Outside of academia, I am a lifelong dancer and movement educator based in Fort Collins, Colorado. I specialize in the Gyrotonic® and Gyrokinesis® methods. My passion lies in sharing the art and science of movement for both rehabilitative and recreational purposes.
